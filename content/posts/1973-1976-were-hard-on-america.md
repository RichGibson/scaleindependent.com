"From 1973-1976 America suffered more wounds to its' ideal of itself than at just about any other time in its' history. Criminal president, oil shock, inflation, military defeat, etc..."

<!--more-->

Apparently from <a href="http://www.rickperlstein.net/the-invisible-bridge/excerpt/">Rick Perlstein</a>

That was exactly when I formed a substantial piece of my identity as an American. Couple it with my mom getting divorced, and having a new boyfriend, and starting to spend time at Ed Burke's house, and my ongoing reading, lots of polyannish science fiction-we call that 'solutionist' now - the idea that we have solutions.

And I still (don't tell anyone) believe in solutions. But still there are those wounds to our ideal of self.	

Updated 2024-07-24

Goodness! Stumbling on this today. Where (throws up hands) everything! But now it doesn't seem like
we are suffering wounds to our ideal of ourself as a nation. We are suffering the wounds of having
some pretty disparate ideas of what our ideals are and should be.
